About Kaamulan Festival

The Spirit of the Kaamulan Festival

"Kaamulan" comes from the Binukid word "amul" which means "to gather". It is a gathering of Bukidnon tribespeople for various purposes - a datuship ritual, wedding, thanksgiving festival, peace pact, or all of these combined.

Showcasing the unique indigenous culture of Bukidnon, the Kaamulan is held annually in Malaybalay City from the second half of February to March 10, marking the province's foundation day in 1917.

The festival honors the customs and traditions of Bukidnon's seven tribes. Dressed in intricate traditional attire adorned with beads, headdresses, and amulets, tribal members gather to dance, chant, perform rituals, and compete in indigenous sports.

Kaamulan is marked by authentic ceremonies such as the Pangampo (worship), Tagulambong hu Datu (chieftain installation), Panumanod (spirit calling), Panlisig (driving away evil), and Pamalas (atonement). Each ritual reflects the deep spiritual and cultural roots of Bukidnon's people.

Umayamnon

Modern-day celebrations include the Civic Parade, Street Dancing Competition, Laga Ta Bukidnon beauty pageant, Ethnic Dance Clinic, and historical reenactments. These events blend tradition with modern engagement.

The festival also promotes local pride through the Garden Show, Agri-F Fair, Kaamulan Bazaar, and various competitions like Motocross, Rodeo, Boxing, and Adventure Races. It serves as a platform for both cultural preservation and economic promotion.
